Private · All pathways Dundee, Scotland

Over £100k of referrals a month, and the UK’s lowest cataract fees.

Accord Eye Care, a private ophthalmology practice in Dundee, uses Lutra to capture referrals and post-operative assessments from more than 30 community optometry practices. The practice opened in January 2026 and has since built a trusted referral network across the community optometry sector, now receiving over £100,000 of referrals through Lutra each month. By collecting patients’ pre-operative information up front, Accord triages faster, runs a leaner practice and keeps its prices the lowest in the country.

The challenge

Low fees leave no room for waste.

Accord Eye Care runs on the lowest cataract fees in the UK. That is a deliberate position, and it is a hard one to hold. Every unnecessary pre-operative visit, every form filled in twice and every patient travelling into central Dundee for an assessment adds cost to the practice.

To keep prices low without cutting clinical quality, Accord needed to take steps out of the pathway. The information that drives triage, the pre-operative data and the post-operative checks all had to be gathered earlier, closer to the patient and with less repetition.

The solution

Capture everything in the community.

Since January 2026, Accord has used Lutra to connect with community optometry across the region. More than 30 optometry practices now send referrals and post-operative assessments straight into Accord through Lutra, replacing the back-and-forth that used to sit between practice and clinic.

Patients complete their pre-operative subjective information before they arrive, so the team can triage and prepare each case in advance. The same channel runs in reverse: Accord offers advice and guidance to local optometrists, helping them manage their own patients and deciding together which cases genuinely need to come in.

Lutra also creates something most referral systems do not: a genuine two-way connection between Accord and the practices that refer to it. Community optometrists do not simply send patients in and lose sight of them. Through Lutra, they receive feedback on each referral, track patients through the pathway, complete post-operative assessments and raise advice and guidance queries directly with Accord. That keeps optometrists connected to their patients’ whole journey and is what has made Accord a practice community optometrists actively want to work with.

Charles Solanki (Lutra Health) and John Ellis (Accord Eye Care).

The outcome

More than £100k of referrals a month, on the same low fees.

Accord now receives over £100,000 of referrals each month through Lutra. That revenue flows directly from the network of more than 30 community optometrists the practice has built since opening in January 2026. The same network delivers an efficiency gain: pre-operative information collected in the community speeds triage and reduces the cost of seeing each patient, which is what lets Accord hold the lowest cataract fees in the UK.

Post-operative assessments captured in the community keep Accord compliant with the national ophthalmic database while sparing patients repeat trips into central Dundee. Rather than travelling in for a hospital visit, a patient simply calls into their local optometry practice, and the result flows back to Accord through Lutra.
