The product

What Lutra Connect is

Lutra Connect is a secure clinical communication platform that links community optometrists to consultant ophthalmologists. It is the connective layer between optometry and ophthalmology: the place where the two sides of an eye care referral can talk to each other, share images and clinical information and keep a proper record of every decision.

In practice it carries four things between the two professions, all held in one auditable record:

Advice and Guidance

An optometrist can ask a vetted consultant about a case and get a structured answer in minutes or hours rather than the weeks a clinic slot can take.

Referrals

Structured and two-way, so the optometrist can see the triage decision and follow what happens to the patient in secondary care.

Surgical and post-operative information

The optometrist can see which implant a patient received and manage follow-up, while the consultant receives structured results suitable for the National Ophthalmic Database.

PROMs and PREMs

The outcome and experience measures private practices need to meet PHIN requirements, collected and formatted as part of the same record.

That single record matters because most of this collaboration currently happens on WhatsApp, personal email and fax. Under ICO guidance, sharing patient data that way is a personal data breach and the liability sits with the individual clinician. Lutra replaces those informal channels with infrastructure built for the job.

The reason

Why Lutra exists

Lutra was founded by ophthalmologists and built by optometrists and ophthalmologists together. Over years working in this market we have learned the same thing from both sides: optometrists and ophthalmologists want to collaborate more closely, but the means of doing so are lacking.

When an optometrist sees something that needs a specialist view, the natural response is to ask a colleague who can help. For years that has meant whatever channel is nearest to hand: a photo over WhatsApp, a call between clinicians who happen to know each other, a faxed referral. The instinct to collaborate is right, but there has been nowhere safe to do it. Patient images end up on personal phones, there is no record of who advised what and the liability for data protection and clinical decisions falls on the individual clinician rather than on a system built to carry it.

We did not want to remove that collaboration. We wanted to keep it and make it safe, so it is secure, structured, auditable and open to every clinician rather than only those with the right contacts. Better collaboration between optometry and ophthalmology means better outcomes for patients, and Lutra handles the compliance work in the background so clinicians can get on with the part that matters.

"Good care depends on optometry and ophthalmology talking openly, with a proper record of every decision. For too long that conversation has happened on the wrong channels, or not at all. So we built Lutra Connect."

Conor Ramsden, Co-founder and Consultant Ophthalmologist

The market

Who we are targeting and what we have learned

We are focused on two buyers, private ophthalmology surgeries and optometry practices, and on three audiences whose attention we want: optometrists, consultant ophthalmologists and the managers who run private hospitals and surgery groups.

We have spent years listening to the eye care market. The clear finding is that clinicians on both sides want more collaboration between ophthalmology and optometry, but there remains little digital infrastructure to support it. What is missing is real two-way communication. Lutra Connect provides it across four areas.

Referrals

Optometrists typically send referrals into a black hole and get no feedback. Lutra lets them see ophthalmology triage decisions and understand what is happening to their patients once they reach secondary care.

Surgical information and post-operative assessments

Optometrists can see which implants a patient has received and manage post-operative care accordingly. Ophthalmology in turn receives structured post-operative results that can be submitted to the National Ophthalmic Database.

Advice and Guidance

Compliant and secure two-way advice and guidance, fully auditable. It removes the risk of informal systems such as WhatsApp, where the clinician carries the liability.

PROMs and PREMs

Critical outcome and experience data that supports the running of a private practice and helps it comply with PHIN requirements.

Coming soon

Automated invoicing and payments Roadmap

Automated invoicing and payments to track the post-operative work optometrists complete. Describe this as roadmap rather than current capability.

The message that lands

The hook for each audience

One hook per audience, with the tension it speaks to, the points that support it and a story angle you can pitch. The common thread is money. Each audience adopts Lutra because it improves their economics, whether that is affordable specialist access, more private work or a measurable referral funnel.

Optometrists

"A consultant ophthalmologist on call, for £50 a month."

Optometrists regularly see cases where they are unsure, where the options today are a long NHS referral or their own best guess. Lutra gives them a vetted consultant ophthalmologist to ask, with an answer back the same day, for a flat fee of £50 a month. That affordable specialist access is the reason an optometrist signs up. The two-way referrals and post-op assessments come included. Both are AI-assisted, so there is less double-keying and each one takes less time. The feedback on what happened to a referred patient is a genuine bonus, but the consultant access is what they are paying for.

  • Same-day Advice and Guidance from vetted consultant ophthalmologists, for £50 a month
  • A specialist opinion on cases that would otherwise mean a long referral or guesswork
  • Referrals and post-op assessments are AI-assisted, cutting double-keying and the time each one takes
  • Coming soon: automated invoicing, so optometrists are paid promptly for the post-operative work they do

Suggested angle: affordable specialist access on the high street. What it means for a patient to get a consultant view without joining an NHS queue and what that access costs a practice, about the price of two sight tests a month.

Ophthalmologists

"More private referrals and faster payment for the work you do."

Consultants build private practice on referral relationships, but generating that work and getting paid for it is fragmented and admin-heavy. Lutra brings Advice and Guidance and referrals that turn into private patients, makes post-operative collaboration with optometry straightforward and is adding automated invoicing so payment is quicker and easier. Working this way also builds rapport with the optometrists who refer, so the relationship is a lasting one rather than transactional. The pitch to a consultant is about revenue and how quickly they can collect it.

  • Advice and Guidance and referrals that bring private patients to you
  • Post-operative assessments shared with optometry, so follow-up is easier to run
  • Coming soon: automated invoicing and payments, so getting paid is quick and low on admin
  • One private channel for the optometrists you choose to work with, run from your desk

Suggested angle: how consultants grow private practice through a digital referral relationship with optometry and take the friction out of getting paid.

Private hospital managers

"A new referral funnel from optometry into your surgeons."

Private eye care groups compete for elective volume, cataract above all, and need to keep consultant and theatre capacity full. The referral relationships that feed that volume are usually informal and unmeasured. Lutra turns them into a measurable funnel from a network of local optometrists into your surgeons, which means more activity and more revenue. Giving consultants better tools to collaborate with optometry also builds rapport, so the funnel is a durable relationship rather than a transactional one. As an added benefit it captures the outcome data that PHIN and the National Ophthalmic Database require, so compliance comes out of the same workflow.

  • A measurable funnel of referrals from local optometry into your consultants and theatres
  • More elective volume, cataract above all, from relationships you can see and track
  • PROMs and PREMs captured for PHIN at response rates above 80%, with post-op data for the National Ophthalmic Database
  • Post-operative care shared with optometry to free clinic capacity, with PMS integration and reporting

Suggested angle: how independent eye care providers grow and measure their referral base. Use cataract demand, independent-sector growth and GIRFT pressure as the news peg, with PHIN and NOD compliance as the secondary benefit.
