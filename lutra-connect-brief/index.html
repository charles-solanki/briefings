02 / Why it has to exist

Six reasons Connect needs to be in market, and why now.

Each point below is independently true, and on its own would justify a product. Stacked together, they describe a category we believe Lutra is uniquely positioned to define: the connective infrastructure of eye care, designed by clinicians, deployed clinician to clinician, and trusted with patient data the way any clinical system has to be.

01

The missing layer between primary and secondary eye care.

Eye care runs on WhatsApp threads, faxed referrals, phone tag and informal networks of consultants doing favours. It works, until it doesn't. Connect is the layer that has been missing, secure and structured and two-way, auditable from the first message to the final outcome.

The connective layer
02

The patient's third path.

Until now, a patient with a concerning finding faced two choices: wait weeks or months on an NHS list, or pay several hundred pounds for a private consultation. Connect offers a third route, fast specialist input at a fraction of the private cost, delivered through the optometrist the patient already trusts.

New service for patients
03

Compliance, not WhatsApp.

Sharing a patient image on WhatsApp is, per the ICO, a personal data breach. The clinician is the data controller, not Meta. Most optoms and surgeons do not realise the informal channel they rely on every day is a regulatory liability in their pocket. Connect is the secure replacement: encrypted, audit-logged, attributable.

Auditable by design
04

Vetted, indemnified, on the record.

Every consultant on Connect is vetted by us and indemnified for the advice they give. That is a meaningful reduction in clinical and medico-legal liability for the optometrist asking the question, and a clean commercial proposition for the consultant answering it. The roster is curated, sub-specialty routed, and reputation-bearing in both directions.

Liability reduced
05

A revenue stream the optom practice did not have.

Today, an optom who spots something concerning has nothing more to sell beyond the eye exam itself. With Connect, the practice can offer the patient, including an NHS patient, same-day specialist input for a defined fee. The practice retains a margin on every case. The patient gets reassurance without a referral.

Practice revenue

The full shared-care stack.

OCT, fundus and visual fields on the clinical thread. PROMs and PREMs collected from the patient, formatted for NOD and PHIN, ready to export.

05 / Defensibility

A two-sided marketplace of real, vetted clinicians. Hard to copy your way past.

Connect is not another point solution that an AI agent can replicate over a weekend. It is a regulated, two-sided clinical marketplace, and the moat is the people in it.

On one side: optometry practices offering a new specialist-input service to their patients. On the other: ophthalmologists offering a new private-pay channel to optometrists. Both vetted by us. Both indemnified. Both jurisdictionally appropriate. Trust accrues to the network, not the software.

Geography is not a constraint. The architecture that works for a UK optom routing to a London consultant works for an Australian optom routing to a Sydney specialist, or an Indian optom routing to a Delhi-based ophthalmologist. From day one, Connect is built to onboard, verify and route across borders, within the regulatory framework of each market we activate.

Why this is the moat: a competing piece of software can be built quickly. A trusted, vetted, indemnified, multi-jurisdictional clinical roster cannot, at least not cheaply, and not in any time horizon that lets a fast-follower catch up. The network is the product.
